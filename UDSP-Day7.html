Bölüm 1 — Kelime Bilgisi
VOCABULARY
7 Soru
For questions 1–7, choose the option that best completes the sentence.
1
The urban heat island effect occurs when cities become significantly warmer than their surrounding rural areas, largely because impermeable surfaces such as concrete and asphalt - - - - heat rather than reflecting it.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) dağıtmak / yaymak
B) emmek / absorbe etmek
C) düzenlemek / kontrol etmek
D) üretmek / oluşturmak
E) aktarmak / iletmek
The mechanism described is that concrete and asphalt hold onto heat — they take it in rather than sending it away. 'Absorb' (B) = to take in and retain energy, the standard term for how surfaces retain solar radiation rather than reflecting it. 'Dissipate' (A) = to scatter or release heat — the opposite of what causes the urban heat island. 'Regulate' (C) = to control, which is neutral and doesn't describe accumulation. 'Generate' (D) = to produce heat — surfaces don't produce heat, they absorb it from the sun. 'Transfer' (E) = to move from one place to another, not to retain.
2
The government's decision to fast-track the construction of a motorway through a protected archaeological site was widely condemned as an - - - - act of institutional negligence.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) münferit / tekil
B) aşırı / utanç verici derecede kötü
C) muğlak / belirsiz
D) kasıtsız / farkında olmadan yapılan
E) daha önce görülmemiş / emsalsiz
Bulldozing a protected archaeological site is described as being 'widely condemned' — this signals the act was seen as shockingly bad. 'Egregious' (B) = outstandingly bad, remarkably offensive — the precise word for something so wrong it attracts widespread condemnation. 'Isolated' (A) = occurring alone, which describes frequency, not the nature of the act. 'Ambiguous' (C) = unclear — but a condemned act is clearly wrong. 'Inadvertent' (D) = accidental — but 'widely condemned' implies deliberate negligence, not accident. 'Unprecedented' (E) = never done before — might partially fit but doesn't explain the condemnation.
3
Carbon capture technology aims to remove CO₂ directly from the atmosphere, but critics argue that it risks providing a - - - - for continued fossil fuel use rather than incentivising genuine emissions reductions.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) ikame / alternatif
B) gerekçe / dayanak
C) çözüm / deva
D) bahane / örtmece gerekçe
E) caydırıcı / engel
Critics worry the technology will be used as a false justification to keep burning fossil fuels without genuine change. 'Pretext' (D) = a false reason given to justify an action while concealing the real motivation — exactly what critics fear: 'we can still use fossil fuels because we have carbon capture'. 'Substitute' (A) = a replacement — carbon capture doesn't replace fossil fuels in this argument. 'Rationale' (B) = a logical reason — similar to pretext, but 'rationale' doesn't carry the negative connotation of a deceptive or hollow justification. 'Remedy' (C) = a cure or solution. 'Deterrent' (E) = something that discourages — the opposite of what critics fear.
4
The fashion industry's rapid shift towards disposable clothing has dramatically - - - - the volume of textile waste entering landfills, raising urgent questions about the sector's environmental accountability.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) sınırlamak / azaltmak
B) sabitlemek / istikrara kavuşturmak
C) tersine çevirmek
D) kısmak / azaltmak
E) şiddetlendirmek / daha da kötüleştirmek
Disposable clothing means more clothes are thrown away more often — this increases, not decreases, waste. 'Exacerbated' (E) = made a problem worse or more severe, which correctly describes how fast fashion has worsened the waste problem. 'Limited' (A) and 'curtailed' (D) both mean reduced — opposite of what fast fashion does to waste. 'Stabilised' (B) = kept at the same level, which ignores the dramatic increase described. 'Reversed' (C) = turned in the opposite direction — would imply waste decreased, the opposite.
5
The discovery that antibiotic-resistant bacteria can transfer resistance genes to previously - - - - strains within hours has dramatically altered scientists' understanding of how resistance spreads.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) baskın / egemen
B) duyarlı / etkilenebilir
C) izole edilmiş / tecrit edilmiş
D) zararlı / tehlikeli
E) aktif / faal
Resistance genes being transferred to strains that previously had no resistance — those strains were vulnerable to antibiotics before. 'Susceptible' (B) = easily affected or harmed, the medical term for bacteria that can be killed by antibiotics. 'Dominant' (A) = most prevalent — a dominant strain already exists; the news is about strains that couldn't resist. 'Isolated' (C) = separated from others — unrelated to resistance status. 'Harmful' (D) = causing damage — bacteria being harmful is not the point; susceptibility to antibiotics is. 'Active' (E) = functioning — unrelated to resistance.
6
The international community's failure to reach a - - - - agreement on genetic privacy standards has left individuals vulnerable to the commercial exploitation of their DNA data across different legal jurisdictions.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) ön / hazırlık niteliğinde
B) tartışmalı / ihtilaf konusu olan
C) gönüllülük esasına dayalı
D) bağlayıcı / zorunlu kılıcı
E) geçici / kısa süreli
Without a legally enforceable international agreement, companies can exploit DNA data freely — the problem is that any existing frameworks are optional. 'Binding' (D) = legally obligating all parties — the absence of a binding agreement is precisely what allows exploitation across jurisdictions. 'Preliminary' (A) = initial or preparatory — a preliminary agreement already exists wouldn't be described as a 'failure to reach' one. 'Controversial' (B) = disputed — an agreement being controversial doesn't explain jurisdictional gaps. 'Voluntary' (C) = optional — a voluntary agreement would actually describe the problem, not the solution. 'Temporary' (E) = short-term — unrelated to the jurisdictional exploitation problem.
7
Preserving a heritage site's - - - - often proves more challenging than restoring its physical structures, as the traditions, languages, and practices associated with a place can disappear without any visible trace.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) finansman / kaynak
B) peyzaj / manzara
C) yasal statü
D) somut olmayan miras / yaşayan miras
E) mimari özellikler
The sentence contrasts physical structures (restorable) with traditions, languages, and practices (harder to preserve). This contrast points to 'intangible heritage' (D) — the non-physical aspects of cultural heritage such as practices, knowledge, and traditions. 'Funding' (A) = financial resources — not related to the physical vs non-physical contrast. 'Landscape' (B) = physical surroundings — which can be seen and measured. 'Legal status' (C) = classification under law — irrelevant to the preservation challenge described. 'Architectural features' (E) = physical aspects of a building — which the sentence explicitly contrasts with the more challenging element.
Bölüm 2 — Dil Bilgisi
GRAMMAR
7 Soru
For questions 8–14, choose the answer that best completes each sentence grammatically.
8
The offshore wind farm - - - - for several years before the surrounding marine ecosystem fully recovered from the disruption caused during its construction.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) faaliyet göstermektedir (Present Perfect Continuous)
B) faaliyet gösteriyordu (Past Continuous)
C) faaliyet gösteriyor olacak (Future Perfect Continuous)
D) faaliyet göstermekteydi / göstermişti (Past Perfect Continuous) ✓
E) faaliyet gösterir (Present Simple)
Two past events with a clear sequence: the ecosystem 'fully recovered' = Past Simple. The wind farm's operation began BEFORE that recovery and continued up to that point — this requires Past Perfect Continuous to emphasise the duration of the earlier ongoing activity. 'Had been operating' (D) = was in a state of ongoing operation before the later past event. 'Has been operating' (A) connects to the present, not to a past reference point. 'Was operating' (B) = Past Continuous, which can describe an action in progress at a past moment but doesn't establish the prior duration relative to the recovery. 'Will have been operating' (C) is future. 'Operates' (E) is present simple.
9
- - - - the misinformation had already been widely shared before the correction was published, the damage to public trust proved extremely difficult to reverse.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) Her ne kadar (zıtlık — tam cümle gerektirir)
B) Göz önüne alındığında / -dığı için (neden + önceden var olan durum) ✓
C) Olmadıkça (olumsuz koşul)
D) Koşuluyla (olumlu koşul)
E) Çünkü (neden — anlam bozulur)
The misinformation spreading widely BEFORE the correction explains WHY the damage was hard to reverse — a causal relationship based on a pre-existing situation. 'Given that' (B) = considering the fact that / because — introduces a premise or established fact that explains the consequence. 'Although' (A) would signal contrast — but the widespread sharing and difficulty of reversal are not contrasting, they are causally linked. 'Because' (E) could work grammatically but 'given that' better captures the sense of an established situation leading to a consequence. 'Unless' (C) = if not — introduces a negative condition, illogical here. 'Provided that' (D) = on condition that — also a conditional, not causal.
10
The report's authors, - - - - findings contradicted several long-held assumptions in the field, were initially met with considerable resistance from established researchers.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) ki onlar (özne ilgi zamiri — kişiler)
B) kimin / -nın (iyelik ilgi zamiri) ✓
C) ki o (nesneler için)
D) o ki (tanımlayan yan cümlelerde, virgülsüz)
E) kimi (nesne ilgi zamiri)
'Findings' belong to the authors — this is a possessive relationship requiring 'whose' (B): 'the authors' findings contradicted...' = 'whose findings contradicted...'. 'Who' (A) would be the subject of a verb — but 'findings' is a noun, not a verb following 'who'. 'That' (D) cannot be used in non-defining clauses set off by commas. 'Which' (C) refers to things, not people. 'Whom' (E) is the object form and cannot introduce a possessive clause.
11
Fast fashion companies are frequently criticised for - - - - suppliers in developing countries to cut costs at the expense of workers' rights and environmental standards.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) baskı uygulamak (-ing gerund — 'for' sonrası doğru) ✓
B) baskı uygulamak (to-infinitive — preposition sonrası yanlış)
C) baskıya maruz kalmak (passive gerund — anlam tersine döner)
D) baskı uygulamış olmak (tamamlanmış eylem — yanlış)
E) baskılanmış (past participle — yanlış yapı)
After a preposition ('for'), English requires the gerund (-ing form), never the infinitive. 'Criticised for + doing' = 'pressurising' (A). 'To pressurise' (B) is the infinitive — grammatically impossible after a preposition. 'Being pressurised' (C) is passive — would mean the companies are being pressurised by suppliers, reversing the intended meaning. 'To have pressurised' (D) is a perfect infinitive — also impossible after a preposition. 'Pressurised' (C) is a past participle — cannot follow 'for' here.
12
- - - - been given greater investment at an earlier stage, the carbon capture technology might have reached commercial viability several years sooner.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) eğer olmuşsa (Present Perfect koşul — yanlış)
B) olması gerekse (Should inversion — First Conditional için)
C) olmuş olsaydı (Had inversion — Third Conditional, 'if' düşürülmüş) ✓
D) olacak olsaydı (Were inversion — Second Conditional için)
E) olmuş olsaydı bile (Even if — 'if' düşürülmemiş)
'Might have reached commercial viability' is the result of a Third Conditional (unreal past). The condition 'if it had been given greater investment' becomes 'Had it been given...' when 'if' is dropped and 'had' inverts with the subject. 'Had it' (C) correctly starts this inverted Third Conditional. 'If it has' (A) = Present Perfect condition, not Third Conditional. 'Should it have' (B) = inverted First Conditional for unlikely but possible future events. 'Were it to have' (D) = inverted Second Conditional for hypothetical present. 'Even if it had' (E) keeps 'if' — the sentence requires inversion (dropping 'if').
13
The cultural heritage committee insisted that the restoration work - - - - overseen by an independent panel of specialists before any phase could be signed off.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) olur (Present Simple — subjunctive yerine yanlış)
B) oldu (Past Simple — resmi talep yapısında zayıf)
C) olarak (gerund/participle — yanlış yapı)
D) olsun (subjunctive base form — resmi talep yapısı) ✓
E) olmak (to-infinitive — 'that' cümlesinde yanlış)
After verbs of insistence, demand, or formal requirement (insist, demand, require, stipulate), formal English uses the bare infinitive subjunctive in a 'that' clause: 'insist that + subject + BASE VERB'. 'Be' (C) is the correct base form regardless of tense or subject. 'Is' (A) = Present Simple — doesn't match the formal mandate structure. 'Was' (B) = Past Simple — would make it a report of fact rather than a requirement. 'Being' (C) = gerund/participle, which cannot head a 'that' clause. 'To be' (E) = infinitive with 'to' — not used in subjunctive that-clauses.
14
The genetic privacy legislation, - - - - passage through parliament had been delayed by fierce lobbying from the biotechnology sector, finally received royal assent last month.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) o ki (tanımlayan yan cümlelerde, virgülsüz)
B) ki o (nesneler — iyelik için yanlış)
C) ki o (kişiler için — yasa için yanlış)
D) ki onun (of which — daha az doğal bu bağlamda)
E) kimin / -nın (iyelik ilgi zamiri) ✓
'Passage' belongs to the legislation — this is possessive. 'Whose' (E) = the legislation's passage was delayed. 'That' (A) cannot be used in non-defining relative clauses (separated by commas). 'Which' (B) could refer to the legislation, but 'which passage' would mean 'which passage of the legislation' — ungrammatical; 'whose passage' is the possessive form. 'Who' (C) refers to people, not laws. 'Of which' (D) could form 'the passage of which had been delayed' — grammatically possible but far less natural and considerably more formal than 'whose'.

10 Soru
For questions 15–24, choose the word or expression that best fits each blank.
15
Misinformation tends to travel faster than accurate information on social media platforms. - - - -, emotionally arousing content — particularly material that triggers outrage or fear — is shared far more widely than neutral or factual posts.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
B) Bunun aksine (zıtlık)
C) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
D) Tam aksine (doğrudan zıtlık)
E) Daha spesifik olarak / Özellikle ✓
The first sentence makes a general claim (misinformation spreads faster). The second specifies a particular reason why — emotional content. 'Specifically' (E) introduces a more precise explanation or example of the general statement. 'Nevertheless/In contrast/On the contrary' (A, B, E) all signal opposition — but the two sentences are in agreement (both about misinformation spreading). 'As a result' (C) would suggest the emotional sharing is a consequence of misinformation being faster, not an explanation of it.
16
Offshore wind farms generate electricity without producing carbon emissions during operation. - - - -, the manufacturing and installation of turbines does require significant energy input, much of which currently comes from fossil fuels.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
B) Benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
C) Ancak / Bununla birlikte (zıtlık) ✓
D) Yani (yeniden ifade)
E) Üstelik / Bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
Offshore wind is zero-emission during operation (positive) but manufacturing requires fossil fuels (a qualifying limitation). 'However' (C) introduces this concessive contrast between the operational benefit and the production-phase cost. 'Consequently' (A) would imply the fossil fuel use is caused by the zero emissions — illogical. 'Similarly' (B) would say both sentences make the same kind of point — they don't. 'That is' (D) would be a restatement — but the second sentence introduces new contradictory information. 'Furthermore' (E) adds more positive information, but the second sentence is a limitation.
17
Carbon capture technologies can theoretically remove large quantities of CO₂ from the atmosphere. - - - -, current versions of the technology are expensive, energy-intensive, and have yet to be demonstrated at the scale necessary to make a material difference to global emissions.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Buna ek olarak (ekleme)
B) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
C) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
D) Yani / Başka bir deyişle (yeniden ifade)
E) Ancak pratikte / Gerçekte ise (zıtlık + pratik gerçeklik vurgusu) ✓
The first sentence states a theoretical possibility; the second reveals the practical reality falls short. 'In practice, however' (E) perfectly captures this contrast between theory and practice — 'in practice' signals the real-world situation, and 'however' signals the contrast with what was just said. 'In addition' (A) adds more of the same — but the second sentence contradicts the first. 'For this reason/As a result' (B, D) imply causation — the practical limitations are not caused by the theoretical capacity. 'That is to say' (D) = a restatement — but the second sentence adds a qualification, not a repetition.
18
Fast fashion brands frequently market their products as affordable and accessible, - - - - the true cost of low prices is borne by garment workers who earn poverty-level wages and by ecosystems damaged by toxic textile dyes.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) göz önüne alındığında (neden/koşul)
B) o kadar ki / böylece (sonuç)
C) her ne kadar / -e rağmen (zıtlık) ✓
D) koşuluyla (olumlu koşul)
E) ihtimale karşı (ihtiyati koşul)
Brands market clothes as affordable (positive framing) but the real cost is paid by workers and ecosystems (hidden negative reality). 'Even though' (C) signals this contrast between the marketing claim and the true situation. 'Given that' (A) introduces a reason — but the real cost is not the reason for the marketing claim. 'So that' (B) introduces purpose or result. 'Provided that' (D) introduces a condition. 'In case' (E) introduces a precautionary action.
19
Antibiotic resistance is often framed as a future threat, - - - - the World Health Organisation already classifies it as one of the greatest current threats to global health.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
B) oysa / -a karşın (iki farklı durum arasında zıtlık) ✓
C) buna ek olarak (ekleme)
D) sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
E) göz önüne alındığında (neden/koşul)
The common perception frames resistance as a future problem; the WHO says it's already a current crisis. 'Whereas' (B) = in contrast, while — draws a direct parallel contrast between two different characterisations of the same issue. 'Therefore/As a result' (A, B) suggest the WHO classification is a consequence of the framing — illogical. 'In addition' (C) would add complementary information, not a contrasting perspective. 'Given that' (E) would introduce a reason or established fact, not a contrast.
20
Rebuilding a war-damaged heritage site to its pre-conflict state raises difficult questions. - - - -, is a reconstructed building the same heritage site, or merely an aesthetically similar replica that lacks the original's historical authenticity?
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) Benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
B) Üstelik / Bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
C) Örneğin ✓
D) Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
E) Hepsinden önemlisi (öncelik/vurgu)
The first sentence makes a general claim (raises difficult questions). The second gives a specific example of one such difficult question. 'For instance' (C) introduces a concrete example of the general point. 'Similarly' (A) would compare this to something else mentioned earlier. 'Moreover' (B) would add another separate point. 'Consequently' (D) would make the philosophical question a result of the rebuilding — illogical. 'Above all' (E) = most importantly, implying this is the most significant of several points already made.
21
The illusory truth effect is most pronounced when people are exposed to repeated claims in low-concentration settings. - - - -, it tends to diminish when individuals are explicitly warned that they may encounter misleading information.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) Üstelik / Bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
B) Buna karşılık / Aksine (zıtlık) ✓
C) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
D) Yani (yeniden ifade)
E) Örneğin (örnekleme)
The first sentence describes when the illusory truth effect is strong. The second describes a condition under which it weakens. 'By contrast' (B) = in opposition to what was just said — correctly signals the reversal from 'most pronounced' to 'tends to diminish'. 'Furthermore' (A) would add another situation where it is strong. 'Therefore' (C) would make the diminishing a consequence of the repeated claims — illogical. 'In other words' (D) would restate the first sentence, but the second sentence is new contrasting information. 'For example' (E) would introduce an example of the effect, not its reversal.
22
Floating offshore wind turbines can be deployed in waters too deep for fixed-bottom installation. - - - -, they open up entirely new geographic areas for wind energy development, including coastlines previously considered unsuitable.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
B) Buna karşılık (zıtlık)
C) Yine de (zıtlık)
D) Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
E) Bu sayede / Böylece (sonuç/mekanizma açıklama) ✓
Floating turbines working in deep water directly enables access to new coastal areas — the new geographic access is a direct consequence of the deep-water capability. 'In this way' (E) = as a result of this mechanism, by means of this — correctly links the capability described in the first sentence to the outcome in the second. 'Nevertheless/Even so/Despite this' (A, D, E) all signal contrast — but the two sentences are in agreement (both positive about floating turbines). 'By contrast' (B) would signal opposition — but the second sentence extends, not contradicts, the first.
23
Prebunking — the practice of warning people about misinformation before they encounter it — has shown considerable promise in experimental settings. - - - -, scaling it to reach diverse populations across different cultural and linguistic contexts remains a major challenge.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) Buna göre / Bu doğrultuda (uyum)

Prebunking shows promise in experiments (positive) but scaling it is a major challenge (limiting factor). 'Nonetheless' (C) = despite that promising result, this difficulty remains — a concessive contrast. 'Accordingly' (A) would imply the scaling challenge is consistent with or follows from the promising results — illogical. 'Similarly' (B) would compare to something else. 'That is' (E) would restate the first sentence. 'As a result' (D) would make the scaling challenge a consequence of the experimental promise — illogical.
24
Cultural heritage preservation is often treated as a luxury concern rather than a development priority, - - - - research increasingly shows that heritage tourism and the cultural identity it sustains are significant drivers of economic resilience in local communities.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C

Heritage is treated as a luxury (dismissive attitude) but research shows it's economically valuable (counter-evidence). 'Even though' (C) signals this contrast between the common attitude and the research finding. 'So that' (A) = purpose or result. 'As long as' (B) = conditional. 'Because' (D) would make the research finding the reason for treating heritage as a luxury — backwards and illogical. 'In case' (E) = precautionary condition.
Bölüm 4 — İng→Türkçe Çeviri
ENG→TUR
2 Soru
For questions 25–26, choose the Turkish translation that best reflects the meaning of the English sentence.
25
Unless the international community agrees on enforceable standards for genetic data privacy, biotechnology companies operating across multiple jurisdictions will continue to exploit the gaps between national regulatory frameworks.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A correctly preserves all key elements: (1) 'unless' = 'olmadığı sürece' (negative condition), (2) 'agrees on enforceable standards' = 'uygulanabilir standartlar konusunda mutabık kalmak', (3) 'will continue to exploit' = 'istismar etmeye devam edecektir' (future continuation), (4) 'gaps between national regulatory frameworks' = 'ulusal düzenleyici çerçeveler arasındaki boşluklar'. B says companies WILL NO LONGER exploit the gaps — treats the condition as already met. C says the disagreement leads to legal problems — adds information not in original and misses the 'unless' structure. D uses Second Conditional ('anlaşsaydı...edemezdi') = unreal past/present, but the original uses First Conditional (real future possibility with 'unless'). E says the international community is tolerating the exploitation — changes the meaning.
26
While offshore wind energy has made remarkable strides in cost reduction over the past decade, the challenge of integrating large quantities of intermittent renewable electricity into national grids has yet to be fully resolved.
Açıklama

Passage 1: The Psychology of Misinformation

In an era of unprecedented information abundance, the ability to distinguish reliable from unreliable content has become one of the most consequential cognitive skills a person can possess. Yet research consistently shows that this skill is far less reliable than most people believe themselves to be. A well-documented phenomenon known as the "illusory truth effect" reveals that simply encountering a false claim repeatedly — even without any accompanying argument or evidence — increases the likelihood that people will later judge it to be true. The effect operates largely below conscious awareness: people often cannot identify which claims they have previously seen, yet their judgements of plausibility are influenced by prior exposure nonetheless.

The reasons why misinformation spreads so effectively are increasingly well understood. Emotionally arousing content — particularly material that triggers outrage, fear, or moral indignation — is shared far more widely and rapidly than neutral or corrective information. Misinformation also tends to be structurally simpler than accurate information: it typically offers a clear villain, an unambiguous cause, and a satisfying narrative resolution, whereas accurate accounts of complex events are often uncertain, multi-causal, and resistant to tidy summarisation. These structural advantages mean that misinformation frequently travels faster and further than the corrections that follow it.

Efforts to counter misinformation have revealed that correction alone is rarely sufficient. Research on the "backfire effect" suggested that correcting a person's false belief could sometimes strengthen rather than weaken it, as individuals interpreted contradictory evidence as a threat to their identity or worldview. While subsequent research has cast doubt on the universality of this effect, it remains well established that corrections are most effective when delivered before exposure to misinformation — a strategy researchers term "prebunking" — rather than after false beliefs have already taken root.

Understanding how to build broad societal resilience to misinformation — through education, media literacy initiatives, and platform design — has therefore become an urgent research priority. The challenge, researchers note, is not simply one of providing people with accurate information, but of understanding and working with the cognitive and social mechanisms that determine how information, accurate or otherwise, spreads and sticks.

Passage 2: Offshore Wind Energy — Potential and Challenges

Offshore wind energy has emerged as one of the most promising large-scale responses to the twin challenges of decarbonising electricity generation and meeting growing energy demand. Unlike onshore wind farms, which are constrained by land availability, proximity to residential areas, and the relatively lower and more variable wind speeds found at ground level, offshore installations benefit from stronger, more consistent winds and can be located far enough from coastlines to minimise visual impact and noise concerns. The world's offshore wind capacity has grown dramatically over the past decade, with installations now generating enough electricity to power tens of millions of homes.

The technology itself continues to evolve rapidly. Fixed-bottom turbines, which are anchored to the seabed and currently dominate the industry, are practical in waters up to approximately sixty metres in depth. Floating wind turbines, which are moored rather than fixed, can be deployed in much deeper waters — opening up vast new geographic areas, including the coastlines of countries such as Japan, Norway, and the United States, where shallow continental shelves are limited. Several commercial-scale floating wind projects are now either operational or under construction, though the technology remains considerably more expensive than its fixed-bottom counterpart.

The environmental footprint of offshore wind, while substantially lower than that of fossil fuel generation, is not negligible. Construction activity causes temporary disruption to seabed habitats, and the electromagnetic fields produced by undersea cables have been shown to affect the navigation behaviour of certain species of fish and rays. Over longer time horizons, however, the turbine foundations themselves tend to function as artificial reefs, attracting a range of marine species and sometimes increasing local biodiversity in the surrounding area.

The economic case for offshore wind has strengthened considerably in recent years as manufacturing costs have fallen and installation techniques have improved. In several countries, offshore wind now produces electricity at costs competitive with or lower than those of new gas-fired power stations, though grid integration — ensuring that the intermittent supply from wind aligns with demand — remains a significant technical and economic challenge that will need to be addressed as the share of wind power in national electricity systems continues to grow.
