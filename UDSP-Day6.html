Bölüm 1 — Kelime Bilgisi
VOCABULARY
7 Soru
For questions 1–7, choose the option that best completes the sentence.
1
The ocean's capacity to absorb carbon dioxide — a process that has so far - - - - the full effects of climate change — is expected to decline as seawater becomes more acidic.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) kötüleştirmek
B) hızlandırmak
C) sorgulamak
D) hafifletmek / azaltmak
E) tahmin etmek
The ocean absorbing CO₂ has been slowing down climate change's effects — not worsening or accelerating them. 'Mitigated' (D) = reduced the severity of something, the precise word for a buffer that lessens an impact. 'Worsened' (A) is the opposite. 'Accelerated' (B) would mean speeding up the effects — also opposite. 'Questioned' (C) and 'Predicted' (E) are semantically unrelated to the cause-effect relationship described.
2
The report's conclusion that gig economy workers are routinely denied basic labour protections was both - - - - and widely anticipated by employment law specialists.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) muğlak / belirsiz
B) tartışmalı / itiraz edilen
C) zarar verici / yıkıcı
D) şaşırtıcı olmayan / beklenen
E) erken / vakitsiz
'Both [blank] and widely anticipated' means the two halves must be near-synonymous — something already expected. 'Unsurprising' (D) = not causing surprise, which parallels 'widely anticipated' perfectly. 'Ambiguous' (A) = unclear, which contradicts a clear conclusion. 'Contested' (B) = disputed, but 'widely anticipated' means expected, not disputed. 'Damaging' (C) could fit logically but does not parallel 'anticipated'. 'Premature' (E) = too early, which contradicts 'anticipated'.
3
Space debris orbiting Earth at high velocity poses a - - - - risk to functioning satellites and crewed spacecraft, a threat that space agencies are treating with growing urgency.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) ihmal edilebilir / önemsiz
B) teorik / varsayımsal
C) geçici / kısa süreli
D) önemli / kayda değer
E) öngörülebilir / tahmin edilebilir
'Treating with growing urgency' signals the risk is significant and real. 'Substantial' (D) = considerable in size or importance, which justifies urgent attention. 'Negligible' (A) = too small to matter — contradicts 'growing urgency'. 'Theoretical' (B) = not yet real — also contradicts urgency about actual satellites. 'Temporary' (C) relates to duration, not magnitude of risk. 'Predictable' (E) describes the nature, not the level, of risk.
4
The - - - - of nanoparticles into targeted tumour cells without damaging surrounding healthy tissue represents one of the most promising frontiers of cancer treatment.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) ulaştırma / iletme
B) bertaraf etme / imha etme
C) uzaklaştırma / giderme
D) emme / özümseme
E) reddetme / dışlama
Nanotechnology in cancer treatment works by carrying therapeutic agents to tumour cells — this is described as 'delivery'. 'Delivery' (A) = the act of transporting and depositing therapeutic agents at the target site, the standard medical term. 'Disposal' (B) implies getting rid of something unwanted. 'Removal' (C) means taking something away from a place. 'Absorption' (D) describes a passive biological process from the tumour's perspective. 'Rejection' (E) is what happens when the body resists something.
5
Demographic ageing is likely to - - - - existing healthcare systems in many countries, as a growing proportion of the population will require sustained medical support.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) güçlendirmek / pekiştirmek
B) modernize etmek / yenilemek
C) değiştirmek / yerine geçmek
D) zorlamak / baskı altına almak
E) atlamak / devre dışı bırakmak
A larger elderly population requiring medical support creates pressure on healthcare systems — it puts them under stress. 'Strain' (D) = to make severe demands on a system, causing difficulty, which is the precise word for this kind of structural pressure. 'Strengthen' (A) is the opposite — more demand weakens, not strengthens. 'Modernise' (B) refers to updating, not to pressure. 'Replace' (C) and 'bypass' (E) are semantically unrelated to the idea of a system being put under pressure.
6
The research team published its findings only after - - - - every alternative explanation for the observed correlation between light exposure and melatonin suppression.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) keşfetmek / bulmak
B) reddetmek / kabul etmemek
C) önermek / ileri sürmek
D) dışlamak / elemek
E) güvenmek / dayanmak
Before publishing, good science requires eliminating alternative explanations. 'Ruling out' (D) = to eliminate a possibility after careful consideration — the standard scientific expression. 'Rejecting' (B) is close but typically refers to actively opposing something already proposed, rather than the systematic elimination process. 'Discovering' (A) = finding, not eliminating. 'Proposing' (C) = suggesting new explanations, the opposite of what is needed. 'Relying on' (E) = depending on, semantically opposite to eliminating.
7
The gig economy's rapid expansion has - - - - traditional employment models, forcing regulators to reconsider frameworks that were designed for a very different labour market.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) pekiştirmek / güçlendirmek
B) bozmak / sekteye uğratmak
C) basitleştirmek
D) restore etmek / eski haline getirmek
E) öngörmek / tahmin etmek
The gig economy has changed employment so fundamentally that old regulations no longer fit — this is a classic disruption pattern. 'Disrupted' (B) = to radically change or interrupt the way something works, exactly what forcing regulators to redesign frameworks implies. 'Reinforced' (A) = made stronger — the opposite. 'Simplified' (C) = made less complex — not what's described. 'Restored' (D) = returned to a previous state — opposite direction. 'Predicted' (E) is unrelated.
Bölüm 2 — Dil Bilgisi
GRAMMAR
7 Soru
For questions 8–14, choose the answer that best completes each sentence grammatically.
8
The extent to which bilingualism protects against cognitive decline - - - - more fully understood if researchers could control for socioeconomic and lifestyle differences between groups.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) anlaşılabilirdi (2. Koşullu — şimdiki hipotez) ✓
B) anlaşılmıştır (Present Perfect — gerçek)
C) anlaşılmış olacak (Future Perfect)
D) anlaşılmaktadır (Present Simple — gerçek)
E) anlaşılmıştı (Past Perfect — yanlış zaman)
'If researchers COULD control' (past simple in the if-clause) establishes a hypothetical present condition that is not currently met — a Second Conditional. The main clause requires 'would/could + base verb'. 'Could be' (A) correctly completes this: a hypothetical present outcome that is not currently the case. 'Has been' (B) and 'is' (D) describe real, current states — incompatible with an unmet condition. 'Will have been' (C) is future perfect. 'Had been' (E) belongs to Third Conditional for unreal past.
9
Light pollution's effects on nocturnal wildlife - - - - in the scientific literature for over two decades before policymakers began to take the issue seriously.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) belgelenmiştir (Present Perfect — günümüzle bağ)
B) belgelenmişti (Past Perfect — geçmişteki daha önceki eylem) ✓
C) belgelenmektedir (Present Simple — şimdiki gerçek)
D) belgeleniyordu (Past Continuous — süreç vurgusu, ama sıra göstermez)
E) belgelenecektir (Future Passive)
Two past events with a clear sequence: documentation happened FIRST, then policymakers began to act. 'Before policymakers began' = policymaker action is the later past event (Past Simple). The earlier event — documentation spanning two decades — requires Past Perfect: 'had been documented' (B). 'Have been documented' (A) connects to the present, not to a past reference point. 'Are documented' (C) is present. 'Were being documented' (D) could imply concurrent action rather than prior completion. 'Will be documented' (E) is future.
10
- - - - the clinical trials yield consistently positive results, the new nanotechnology treatment is unlikely to receive regulatory approval within the next three years.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) olmadıkça / eğer...değilse ✓
B) her ne kadar (zıtlık — tam cümle)
C) göz önüne alındığında (neden)
D) olsa bile (tavizli koşul)
E) koşuluyla (olumlu koşul)
The sentence says the treatment is 'unlikely to receive approval' — this is the negative outcome if something does NOT happen. 'Unless' (A) = 'if...not' — 'Unless the trials yield positive results' = 'If the trials do NOT yield positive results, approval is unlikely'. 'Although' (B) signals contrast, not a negative condition. 'Given that' (C) introduces a reason/premise. 'Even if' (D) means approval is unlikely regardless — but the sentence implies positive results WOULD lead to approval. 'Provided that' (E) introduces a positive condition for a positive outcome.
11
The conference delegates, - - - - views on space debris regulation varied considerably, nonetheless agreed on the urgency of establishing a binding international framework.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) ki onlar (özne ilgi zamiri)
B) o ki (tanımlayan yan cümlelerde, virgülsüz)
C) kimin / -nın (iyelik ilgi zamiri) ✓
D) kimi (nesne ilgi zamiri)
E) ki o / -ki (nesneler için)
'Views' belong to the delegates — a possessive relationship requires 'whose' (C). 'Whose views varied' = 'the delegates' views varied'. 'Who' (A) would need to be the subject of a verb: 'delegates who [verb]' — but 'views' is a noun, not a verb. 'Which' (E) refers to things, not people. 'That' (B) cannot be used in non-defining clauses set off by commas. 'Whom' (D) is the object form and cannot introduce a possessive clause.
12
The discovery that the ageing brain retains far greater plasticity than previously assumed - - - - established assumptions about neurological decline and opened new avenues for therapeutic intervention.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) sorguluyor (Present Simple — genel gerçek odaklı)
B) sorgulamıştı (Past Perfect — geçmişteki daha önceki eylem)
C) sorguluyordu (Past Continuous — geçmiş süreç)
D) sorgulayacaktır (Future)
E) sorgulamıştır (Present Perfect — geçmişte başlayıp günümüzü etkileyen) ✓
The discovery has already changed the field — and continues to be relevant to the present. 'Has challenged' (E) = Present Perfect, used for a past event with a lasting effect on the present ('has changed assumptions' and 'has opened avenues'). The parallel structure 'has challenged...and opened' confirms Present Perfect. 'Challenges' (A) = present simple, used for timeless truths, less precise here. 'Had challenged' (B) would require a later past reference point. 'Was challenging' (C) suggests an action in progress at a past moment.
13
Researchers strongly recommend - - - - LED streetlights with warmer-toned alternatives in areas near protected nature reserves to reduce the impact of light pollution on nocturnal species.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) değiştirmeyi (to-inf. — 'recommend' sonrası yanlış)
B) değiştirmek (base form — 'recommend' sonrası yanlış)
C) değiştirilmiş (past participle — yanlış yapı)
D) değiştirmeyi (-ing gerund — 'recommend' sonrası doğru) ✓
E) değiştirmiş olmayı (tamamlanmış eylem — yanlış anlam)
'Recommend' is always followed by the -ing (gerund) form when the same subject is implied: 'recommend + doing something'. 'Replacing' (D) is the correct gerund. 'To replace' (A) is the infinitive — 'recommend' does not take the bare or full infinitive in this construction. 'Replace' (B) is the bare infinitive, also incorrect after 'recommend'. 'Replaced' (C) is a past participle, ungrammatical here. 'To have replaced' (E) implies a completed past action, which contradicts the forward-looking recommendation.
14
- - - - the full economic costs of demographic ageing been factored into long-term fiscal planning, many governments would now be in a considerably stronger budgetary position.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) her ne kadar (zıtlık bağlacı — tam cümle gerektirir)
B) çünkü / -den beri (neden/zaman)
C) olsaydı (inverted Third Conditional — 'if' düşürülmüş) ✓
D) her ne kadar (zıtlık — tam cümle gerektirir)
E) çünkü (neden bağlacı)
This is an inverted Third Conditional — 'if' is omitted and 'had' moves to the front: 'Had the costs been factored in...' = 'If the costs had been factored in, governments would now be stronger'. 'Had' (C) is the only word that correctly creates this formal inversion. The main clause ('would now be') uses Mixed Conditional logic (past condition → present result). 'Although/Even though' (A, D) introduce contrast clauses and cannot create conditional meaning. 'Since/Because' (B, E) introduce causal clauses.
Bölüm 3 — Boşluk Doldurma
CLOZE TEST
10 Soru
For questions 15–24, choose the word or expression that best fits each blank.
15
For decades, scientists assumed that the adult brain was largely fixed in structure. - - - -, research over the past thirty years has demonstrated that the brain retains the capacity to reorganise itself in response to learning and experience throughout a person's lifetime.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
B) Benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
C) Bunun aksine (doğrudan zıtlık)
D) Ancak / Bununla birlikte (zıtlık) ✓
E) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
The first sentence states a past assumption (brain is fixed). The second presents the contrasting reality revealed by recent research. 'However' (D) signals a contrast between what was believed and what was later discovered. 'In contrast' (C) is close, but 'however' is more natural as a bridge between a past belief and its correction. 'Consequently/Therefore' (A, E) would imply the new research is a consequence of the old assumption — illogical. 'Similarly' (B) would imply the new research confirms the assumption.
16
Ocean acidification threatens not only coral reefs but also the broader marine food chain. - - - -, it affects the ability of shellfish, sea urchins, and other organisms to form the calcium carbonate shells they depend on for protection.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Bunun aksine (zıtlık)
B) Ancak (zıtlık)
C) Bunun yerine (alternatif)
D) Aksi takdirde (koşullu sonuç)
E) Daha spesifik olarak / Özellikle ✓
The first sentence makes a broad claim (threatens the food chain broadly). The second gives a specific mechanism of how that harm works (calcium carbonate shell formation). 'Specifically' (E) signals that what follows provides a more precise, concrete example of the general claim just made. 'By contrast' (A) and 'However' (B) signal opposition — but the two sentences agree. 'Instead' (C) introduces an alternative. 'Otherwise' (D) introduces a conditional consequence.
17
The evidence linking artificial light at night to human health disorders is growing; - - - -, it remains difficult to establish conclusive causal relationships because individuals differ in ways beyond their light exposure alone.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) üstelik / bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
B) buna rağmen / yine de (zıtlık) ✓
C) yani / başka bir deyişle (yeniden ifade)
D) buna göre / bu doğrultuda (uyum)
E) sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
The evidence is growing (positive for the field) but causation is hard to prove (limitation). 'Nevertheless' (B) = despite the growing evidence, this difficulty persists — a concessive contrast. 'Furthermore' (A) = adds more supporting evidence, which is not what the second clause does. 'That is' (C) = restates or clarifies. 'Accordingly' (D) = in a way consistent with what was said. 'As a consequence' (E) would imply the difficulty is caused by the growing evidence — illogical.
18
Nanotechnology offers unprecedented precision in drug delivery. - - - -, nanoparticles can be engineered to release their therapeutic payload only when they detect the specific biochemical conditions present in tumour tissue.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) Örneğin ✓
B) Bunun aksine (zıtlık)
C) Ne olursa olsun (koşulsuz)
D) Yine de / Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
E) Alternatif olarak (seçenek)
The first sentence makes a general claim about precision. The second gives a concrete example of what that precision looks like. 'For instance' (A) introduces a specific illustration of the preceding general statement. 'In contrast' (B) signals opposition — the second sentence supports, not contradicts. 'Even so' (D) signals concession. 'Alternatively' (E) would offer a different option. 'Regardless' (C) introduces a condition-free conclusion, inappropriate here.
19
The gig economy has created flexible earning opportunities for millions of workers. - - - -, it has also eliminated many of the legal protections and financial predictability that traditional employment provides.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) Buna ek olarak (ekleme — zıtlık değil)
B) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
C) Yani (yeniden ifade)
D) Aynı zamanda / Öte yandan ✓
E) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
The gig economy creates opportunities (positive) and simultaneously removes protections (negative). 'At the same time' (D) = both things are true simultaneously and in tension — the ideal connector for a statement that introduces a coexisting contradictory reality. 'In addition' (A) would continue the positive list rather than introducing a counterweight. 'As a result/For this reason' (B, E) would imply the loss of protections is caused by the opportunities. 'That is' (C) = restating the same idea.
20
Bilingual children may initially develop their vocabulary in each language more slowly than their monolingual peers. - - - -, when their total vocabulary across both languages is counted together, the gap typically disappears.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
B) Benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
C) Yani / Başka bir deyişle (yeniden ifade)
D) Özellikle (belirginleştirme)
E) Ancak / Bununla birlikte ✓
The first sentence describes a potential disadvantage (slower single-language vocabulary). The second qualifies or overturns this with a clarifying counterpoint (combined vocabulary is equivalent). 'However' (E) introduces this corrective contrast — what seems like a disadvantage disappears under a different measurement. 'Consequently' (A) would make the disappearing gap a result of the slower development. 'Similarly' (B) implies both sentences say the same thing. 'In other words' (C) = restating, but the second sentence adds new information. 'In particular' (D) = narrowing focus.
21
Space agencies have successfully tracked the majority of large debris objects in low Earth orbit. - - - -, millions of smaller fragments — too small to track individually but large enough to cause catastrophic damage — remain unmonitored.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
B) Örneğin (örnek verme)
C) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
D) Üstelik / Bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
E) Buna rağmen / Yine de (zıtlık) ✓
Large debris is tracked (positive, reassuring) but small fragments remain unmonitored (a remaining threat). 'Nevertheless' (E) = despite that tracking success, this significant problem persists. 'Therefore/As a result' (A, C) would make the unmonitored fragments a consequence of the successful tracking — illogical. 'For example' (B) would suggest the unmonitored fragments are an illustration of successful tracking. 'Moreover' (D) would add to the positive point, but the second sentence is a contrasting limitation.
22
Cognitive reserve — the brain's accumulated capacity to tolerate neurological damage — is built up over a lifetime through education, social engagement, and intellectually stimulating activity. - - - -, there are good reasons to believe that lifelong learning may offer meaningful protection against age-related cognitive decline.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) Bunun aksine (zıtlık)
B) Dolayısıyla / Bunun sonucunda ✓
C) Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
D) Öte yandan (zıtlık/alternatif perspektif)
E) Örneğin (örnekleme)
The first sentence explains what builds cognitive reserve (education, social engagement, learning). The second draws a logical conclusion: since these activities build reserve, learning protects against decline. 'Consequently' (B) signals this direct causal logical inference. 'By contrast/Nonetheless/On the other hand' (A, B, D) all signal opposition — but the two sentences are in agreement. 'For instance' (E) would introduce an example rather than a logical conclusion.
23
The assumption that gig workers are self-employed entrepreneurs who freely choose their working conditions has been challenged by multiple court rulings in different countries, - - - - legislators have been slow to update employment law to reflect this new legal reality.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) o kadar ki (sonuç yapısı — o kadar...ki)
B) oysa / -a karşın (zıtlık iki konu arasında)
C) bu yüzden (neden-sonuç)
D) her ne kadar / -e rağmen ✓
E) artık / -diğine göre (zaman/neden)
The assumption has been challenged by courts (progress in legal interpretation) but legislators have been slow to update laws (failure to follow through). 'Even though' (D) = despite the court rulings challenging the assumption, legislators haven't updated the law — a concessive contrast between legal developments and legislative inaction. 'Whereas' (B) typically contrasts two parallel things about different subjects. 'So that' (A) introduces purpose or result. 'Which is why' (C) introduces a consequence — but slow legislation would be the opposite of the expected consequence of court challenges. 'Now that' (E) introduces a reason based on a new situation.
24
Light-emitting diodes have dramatically reduced energy consumption in street lighting, - - - - they have also introduced a new problem: the blue-wavelength light they emit is particularly effective at suppressing melatonin production.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) olduğu sürece (koşul)
B) çünkü (neden)
C) göz önüne alındığında (neden/koşul)
D) koşuluyla (koşul)
E) ancak / fakat (zıtlık bağlacı) ✓
LEDs reduce energy consumption (benefit) but the blue light suppresses melatonin (new problem). 'But' (E) = a simple coordinating conjunction that introduces a contrasting clause — the most natural choice here, where a positive development is immediately followed by an unintended negative consequence. 'As long as/Provided that' (A, D) introduce conditions. 'Because/Given that' (B, C) introduce reasons — but the melatonin suppression is not the reason for the energy saving.

2 Soru
For questions 25–26, choose the Turkish translation that best reflects the meaning of the English sentence.
25
Although the evidence for a cognitive advantage in bilingual speakers has been contested, few researchers now dispute that managing two language systems simultaneously constitutes a genuine and demanding form of mental exercise.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A correctly preserves all three key elements: (1) 'although...contested' = 'tartışmalı olmaya devam etse de' (concession maintained), (2) 'few researchers now dispute' = 'artık pek az araştırmacı...inkâr etmektedir' (near-universal agreement on the exercise point), (3) 'genuine and demanding form of mental exercise' = 'gerçek ve zorlu bir zihinsel egzersiz'. B states the evidence is conclusively proven — contradicts 'contested'. C says researchers agree there is NO advantage — contradicts the original. D says it has now been proven conclusively — contradicts 'contested'. E says the issue is still completely unresolved — contradicts 'few dispute'.
26
Were the international community to agree on a binding framework for space debris removal, the long-term risk of collision cascades that could render entire orbital zones unusable would be substantially reduced.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
'Were...to agree' is an inverted Second Conditional — hypothetical present/future (the agreement hasn't happened yet but is possible). The main clause uses 'would be substantially reduced'. E correctly translates this as a present hypothetical using 'anlaşması halinde...azalabilir' — maintaining possibility without certainty. A uses past conditional ('anlaşmış olsaydı...azalmış olurdu') — this is Third Conditional (unreal past), not Second (hypothetical present). B states the risk has already been eliminated — treats as real. C uses 'anlaşmaya varırsa...azalacaktır' — First Conditional (likely future), stronger certainty than the original 'would be'. D introduces refusal to agree — not in original.
Bölüm 5 — Türkçe→İng Çeviri
TUR→ENG
2 Soru
For questions 27–28, choose the English translation that best reflects the meaning of the Turkish sentence.
27
Demografik yaşlanmanın sağlık sistemlerine getireceği mali yükün tam olarak hesaplanabilmesi için, araştırmacıların hem mevcut bakım maliyetlerini hem de uzun vadeli altyapı yatırımı gereksinimlerini kapsamlı biçimde modellemesi gerekmektedir.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
B correctly preserves all elements: (1) 'hesaplanabilmesi için' = 'in order for...to be fully calculated', (2) 'araştırmacıların...modellemesi gerekmektedir' = 'researchers need to...model', (3) 'hem mevcut bakım maliyetlerini hem de uzun vadeli altyapı yatırımı gereksinimlerini' = 'both current care costs and long-term infrastructure investment requirements'. A says researchers have ALREADY calculated — the Turkish text says it hasn't been done yet. C says it has been thoroughly calculated — contradicts original. D introduces prioritisation advice — not in original. E says burden can only be REDUCED — the original is about calculating the burden, not reducing it.
28
Işık kirliliğinin gece aktif olan hayvanlar üzerindeki biyolojik etkileri giderek daha iyi belgelenmekte olsa da kentsel aydınlatma altyapısının daha uygun alternatiflere dönüştürülmesinin önünde önemli ekonomik engeller bulunmaktadır.

Passage 1: The Science of Bilingualism

For much of the twentieth century, bilingualism was regarded with suspicion in educational circles. Some researchers argued that growing up with two languages would overburden a child's developing mind, leading to confusion, slower vocabulary acquisition, and reduced academic performance. It is now understood that these concerns were largely unfounded, and that the cognitive reality of bilingualism is considerably more nuanced — and in many respects more beneficial — than earlier generations of researchers supposed.

Modern neuroscience has revealed that managing two language systems simultaneously exercises the brain's executive control network — the set of cognitive functions responsible for directing attention, switching between tasks, and suppressing irrelevant information. Because bilingual individuals must constantly monitor and regulate which language is appropriate in a given context, this network is engaged more intensively and more frequently than in monolinguals. Several longitudinal studies have found that this sustained engagement is associated with measurable advantages in tasks requiring divided attention and cognitive flexibility, even in domains that have nothing to do with language itself.

Perhaps the most discussed benefit concerns cognitive ageing. Studies tracking bilingual and monolingual individuals across their lifetimes have found that the onset of dementia symptoms in bilingual speakers occurs, on average, several years later than in monolingual counterparts with equivalent educational backgrounds and health profiles. Researchers caution that bilingualism is not a cure for dementia — the underlying disease progresses at the same rate — but it appears to contribute to what neurologists term "cognitive reserve": the brain's capacity to continue functioning effectively despite the accumulation of age-related damage.

Critics of this research point out that it is methodologically difficult to isolate bilingualism as a variable, since bilingual individuals often differ from monolingual comparison groups in ways that might independently affect cognitive outcomes — including social background, immigration experience, and the types of professional activities they engage in throughout their lives. This debate continues, and while the evidence for a "bilingual advantage" remains contested, few researchers now dispute that managing two languages represents a genuine and sustained form of cognitive exercise.

Passage 2: Light Pollution and Its Consequences

The proliferation of artificial lighting across the globe over the past century has been so pervasive that today, more than eighty percent of the world's population lives under skies so illuminated that the Milky Way is no longer visible to the naked eye. This phenomenon — known as light pollution — has long been treated primarily as an aesthetic concern, a regrettable side effect of progress that deprives urban dwellers of the natural spectacle of a dark sky. Increasingly, however, scientists are documenting its biological and ecological consequences, many of which are far more serious than the loss of a view.

Artificial light at night disrupts the circadian rhythms — the roughly twenty-four-hour biological cycles that regulate sleep, hormone production, metabolism, and immune function — of a vast range of species. For nocturnal animals, whose entire behavioural repertoire evolved in the context of natural cycles of darkness, the consequences can be particularly severe. Artificial lighting can disorient migrating birds, which navigate partly by starlight; attract and disorient insects that are essential pollinators; and alter the behaviour of marine turtles, which rely on the natural brightness of the ocean horizon to navigate from beach nesting sites to open water.

The consequences for human health have also attracted increasing scientific attention. Epidemiological studies have identified associations between chronic exposure to artificial light at night and elevated rates of sleep disorders, obesity, depression, and, in some studies, certain types of cancer. The proposed mechanism involves the suppression of melatonin — a hormone produced primarily during darkness — by exposure to light, particularly in the blue wavelengths emitted by many modern LED sources.

Researchers and urban planners have begun to identify practical responses. Using warmer-toned lighting, installing shields that prevent upward light scatter, and implementing timed switching systems that reduce light output during the hours when nocturnal activity is at its peak are all measures that have been shown to reduce light pollution's impact significantly without requiring wholesale abandonment of outdoor lighting.
