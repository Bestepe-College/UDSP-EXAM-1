For questions 8–14, choose the answer that best completes each sentence grammatically.
8
By the time the new community garden - - - -, more than two hundred local residents had already signed up to take part.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) açılıyor (Present Simple)
B) açılacak (Future)
C) açılmıştır (Present Perfect)
D) açıldığında (Past Simple — zaman zarfı ile) ✓
E) açılıyordu (Past Continuous)
'By the time' refers to a point in the past ('had already signed up' = Past Perfect). In past time clauses with 'by the time', we use Past Simple for the reference point. 'Opened' (C) correctly forms this: 'By the time it opened, residents had already signed up'. 'Opens' (A) is Present Simple — wrong for a past sequence. 'Will open' (B) is future. 'Was opening' (E) is Past Continuous — 'by the time' requires Past Simple for the clause that sets the reference point. 'Has opened' (C) is Present Perfect.
9
The local council - - - - a new park next year, which will replace the unused car park near the town centre.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) inşa ediyor (Present Simple)
B) inşa etti (Past Simple)
C) inşa etmiştir (Present Perfect)
D) inşa ediyordu (Past Continuous)
E) inşa edecek (going to — önceden planlanmış niyet) ✓
'Next year' signals a future plan. 'Is going to build' (E) = a pre-arranged intention or plan decided before this moment — councils make official plans, so 'going to' (prior decision) fits better than 'will' (spontaneous decision). 'Builds' (A) = Present Simple, used for timetable facts, not announced plans. 'Built' (B) = past, contradicts 'next year'. 'Has built' (C) = Present Perfect, for recently completed past actions. 'Was building' (D) = Past Continuous.
10
If the weather - - - - better this weekend, we will hold the volunteer clean-up event in the park.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) olacak (Future — if cümlesinde kullanılmaz)
B) olurdu (would — 2. Koşullu için)
C) olursa (Present Simple — 1. Koşullu) ✓
D) olsaydı (Past Simple — 2. Koşullu için)
E) olmuştur (Present Perfect)
'We WILL hold the event' = First Conditional (real, possible future situation). In First Conditional if-clauses, Present Simple is used to refer to future events — NOT 'will'. 'Is' (C) correctly forms the First Conditional: 'If the weather is better, we will hold the event'. 'Will be' (A) = 'will' cannot appear in an if-clause. 'Would be' (B) = Second Conditional (unreal present). 'Was' (D) = Second Conditional. 'Has been' (E) = Present Perfect.
11
The children were excited about - - - - to the science museum for their end-of-year school trip.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) gitmeye (-ing gerund — preposition 'about' sonrası) ✓
B) gitmek (to-infinitive — preposition sonrası yanlış)
C) gitti (Past Simple — yanlış yapı)
D) gitmiş (past participle — yanlış yapı)
E) gitmek (base form — preposition sonrası yanlış)
After a preposition ('about'), the gerund (-ing form) is always used. 'Going' (A) is the correct gerund form after 'excited about'. 'Go' (E) is the bare infinitive — not used after a preposition. 'To go' (B) is the full infinitive — also not used after a preposition. 'Went' (C) is Past Simple. 'Gone' (D) is past participle — cannot follow 'about' in this structure.
12
This is the first time she - - - - a speech in front of such a large audience.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) veriyor (Present Simple)
B) verdi (Past Simple)
C) vermiştir (Present Perfect — 'this is the first time' yapısı) ✓
D) verecek (Future)
E) veriyordu (Past Continuous)
'This is the first time...' is a fixed structure that requires Present Perfect: 'This is the first time + subject + has/have + past participle'. 'Has given' (C) is correct — it means she has not done this before up to now. 'Gives' (A) = Present Simple, used for habits. 'Gave' (B) = Past Simple — 'this is the first time she gave' is non-standard. 'Will give' (D) = future. 'Was giving' (E) = Past Continuous.
13
The train journey - - - - much longer than usual yesterday because there were engineering works on the main line.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) sürüyor (Present Simple)
B) sürmüştür (Present Perfect)
C) sürmektedir (Present Continuous)
D) sürdü (Past Simple) ✓
E) sürecek (Future)
'Yesterday' is a specific past time marker that requires Past Simple. 'Took' (D) = Past Simple, the only correct tense with 'yesterday'. 'Takes' (A) = Present Simple, for habitual facts. 'Has taken' (B) = Present Perfect, cannot be used with 'yesterday'. 'Is taking' (C) = Present Continuous, for now. 'Will take' (E) = future.
14
The manager asked the team - - - - their reports before the end of the week.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) teslim etmek (base form — dolaylı anlatımda yanlış)
B) teslim etti (Past Simple — yanlış yapı)
C) teslim etmelerini (to-infinitive — ask + object + to-inf.) ✓
D) teslim etmeyi (gerund — yanlış yapı)
E) teslim eden (relative — yanlış yapı)
'Ask someone to do something' = ask + object + to-infinitive. 'To submit' (C) is the correct form: 'asked the team to submit'. 'Submit' (A) = bare infinitive — 'asked to submit' without object is different. 'Submitted' (B) = Past Simple — wrong structure. 'Submitting' (D) = gerund — 'asked submitting' is ungrammatical. 'That submit' (E) = not a standard clause structure.

Passage 1: The Importance of Sleep for Young People

Most teenagers and young adults do not get enough sleep. Research shows that young people between the ages of thirteen and eighteen need between eight and ten hours of sleep each night, but many regularly sleep for only six or seven hours. This lack of sleep can have a serious effect on their daily lives.

When young people do not sleep enough, it becomes harder for them to concentrate in class and remember what they have learned. Their mood can also suffer — students who are tired are more likely to feel anxious or irritable. Some studies have even found a link between poor sleep and lower exam results.

One of the main reasons why teenagers sleep less than they should is the use of electronic devices at night. Looking at screens before bed can delay the release of a hormone called melatonin, which helps the body prepare for sleep. As a result, young people often feel wide awake at midnight, even when they need to be up early the next morning.

Experts suggest several ways to improve sleep habits. Going to bed and waking up at the same time every day — even at weekends — helps the body develop a natural sleep rhythm. Avoiding screens for at least one hour before bed and keeping the bedroom dark and quiet can also make a real difference. Small changes like these, if practised consistently, can lead to significant improvements in both sleep quality and overall wellbeing.

Passage 2: Community Gardens

A community garden is a shared outdoor space where local people grow fruit, vegetables, and flowers together. These gardens have become increasingly popular in cities around the world, and many local governments now support their development by providing land and basic equipment.

Community gardens offer a wide range of benefits. First, they give people who live in flats or houses without gardens the chance to grow their own food. This can help reduce food costs for families and encourage healthier eating habits. Second, spending time outdoors and doing physical work in a garden is good for both mental and physical health. Many people say that gardening helps them feel calmer and more connected to nature.

Perhaps the most important benefit is the sense of community that these gardens create. People from different backgrounds work side by side, share knowledge, and often form lasting friendships. In some cities, community gardens have helped to improve relations between neighbours who previously had little contact with each other.

Despite these advantages, community gardens do face some challenges. Finding suitable land in busy city centres can be difficult and expensive. Keeping the garden active also requires a group of committed volunteers who are willing to give their time regularly. When enthusiasm fades or people move away, gardens can quickly fall into disuse. Successful community gardens tend to be those with a strong organising committee and clear rules about responsibilities.
