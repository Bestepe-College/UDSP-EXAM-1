For questions 8–14, choose the option that correctly completes the sentence grammatically.
8
The scientists announced that they - - - - on the same project for nearly a decade before they finally achieved a breakthrough.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: D) had been working
The sentence describes an ongoing action (working) that happened before another past event (achieving a breakthrough). This requires Past Perfect Continuous: "had been working." The reporting verb "announced" (past) confirms we need a past reference.
A) are working — çalışıyorlar (Present Continuous — wrong tense)
B) have been working — çalışmaktadırlar (Present Perfect Continuous — can't follow "announced")
C) will have worked — çalışmış olacaklar (Future Perfect — wrong)
D) had been working ✓ — çalışmaktaydılar (Past Perfect Continuous — correct)
E) were working — çalışıyorlardı (Past Continuous — no sense of duration before breakthrough)
9
It is essential that every employee - - - - the safety regulations before entering the construction site.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: B) review
After "It is essential/important/necessary/vital that...", English uses the subjunctive mood — the base form of the verb (no -s, no tense). "It is essential that every employee review" — NOT "reviews." This is a formal grammar rule.
A) reviews — inceler (third-person -s — wrong in subjunctive)
B) review ✓ — incelesin (subjunctive base form — correct after "essential that")
C) reviewed — inceledi (past — wrong tense)
D) has reviewed — incelemiştir (Present Perfect — wrong)
E) will review — inceleyecek (Future — wrong after "essential that")
10
The children were made - - - - their vegetables before they were allowed to have dessert.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: E) eat
In passive causative structures: "make someone do" (active) → "be made to do" (active infinitive, passive). BUT "were made eat" without "to" is also correct — "make" in passive takes bare infinitive. Here the correct passive form is "were made to eat" or "were made eat." Since the option is bare "eat," it fits the passive make construction.
A) eating — yemek (-ing form — "make" doesn't take gerund)
B) to have eaten — yemiş olmak (perfect infinitive — too complex)
C) eaten — yenilmiş (past participle — wrong)
D) having eaten — yemiş olarak (participle phrase — wrong)
E) eat ✓ — yemek (bare infinitive — correct in "be made + bare inf")
11
The manager suggested - - - - the meeting until all the necessary reports had been submitted.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: A) postponing
"Suggest" is always followed by a gerund (-ing), never by a to-infinitive. Key verbs + gerund: suggest, recommend, avoid, consider, enjoy, finish, deny, practise, miss, keep, mind, imagine.
A) postponing ✓ — ertelemek (-ing gerund — correct after "suggest")
B) to postpone — ertelemek için (to-infinitive — NOT used after "suggest")
C) postponed — ertelendi (past tense — grammatically impossible here)
D) to have postponed — ertelemiş olmak (perfect infinitive — wrong)
E) postpone — ertelemek (bare infinitive — "suggest" never takes this)
12
The more funding the research centre receives, - - - - it will be able to conduct.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: C) the more experiments
"The more...the more..." is a fixed double comparative structure. Both clauses must start with "the + comparative." More funding → more experiments. The "the" before "more" in the second clause is mandatory.
A) fewer experiments — daha az deney (illogical — more funding = more experiments)
B) less experiments — daha az deney ("less" used with uncountable nouns; "experiments" is countable)
C) the more experiments ✓ — ne kadar çok deney (correct double comparative)
D) the most experiments — en çok deney (superlative — not used in this structure)
E) more experiments — daha çok deney (missing "the" — structure requires "the more")
13
The famous painting - - - - from the museum last night, and the police are currently investigating the theft.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: D) was stolen
The painting cannot steal itself — passive voice is required. "Last night" signals Simple Past. Past Simple Passive = "was/were + past participle." "Was stolen" is the correct form.
A) has stolen — çalmıştır (Present Perfect Active — painting can't steal)
B) is stealing — çalıyor (Present Continuous Active — wrong voice and tense)
C) had been stolen — çalınmıştı (Past Perfect Passive — needs earlier reference)
D) was stolen ✓ — çalındı (Past Simple Passive — correct)
E) stole — çaldı (Past Simple Active — painting can't steal)
14
Both the principal - - - - the teachers agreed that the new curriculum needed significant revision before implementation.
✓ Doğru Cevap & Açıklama
Doğru Cevap: A) and
"Both...and..." is the correct correlative conjunction pair. "Both A and B" means both parties are included. "Neither...nor" is the negative pair. "Both...or" is grammatically incorrect.
A) and ✓ — ve (correct: "both...and" = hem...hem de)
B) nor — ne de ("neither...nor" — wrong pair with "both")
C) or — veya ("either...or" — wrong pair with "both")
D) but — ama (contrast — wrong pair with "both")
E) yet — yine de (contrast/time — wrong pair)

The Placebo Effect

The placebo effect is one of the most intriguing phenomena in medical science. It occurs when a patient experiences a real improvement in their condition after receiving a treatment that has no active medical ingredients — such as a sugar pill or a saline injection. What makes this phenomenon so fascinating is that the improvement is genuine, not imagined. Patients may report reduced pain, decreased anxiety, or even measurable changes in blood pressure, all from a treatment that should have no physical effect whatsoever.

Scientists believe that the placebo effect works primarily through the power of expectation. When a patient believes they are receiving effective treatment, the brain releases natural chemicals such as endorphins and dopamine, which can produce real physiological changes. This explains why the placebo effect tends to be stronger when patients have high confidence in their doctor or when the "treatment" is presented in a convincing and reassuring way.

Interestingly, research has shown that the placebo effect can even work when patients are fully aware that they are receiving a placebo. In these open-label studies, participants who knowingly took sugar pills still reported significant improvements, particularly in conditions such as irritable bowel syndrome and chronic back pain. This suggests that the ritual of taking medicine — the act of receiving care — may itself trigger healing responses in the body.

However, the placebo effect also raises ethical questions. Should doctors use placebos without patients' knowledge? While some argue that if it helps, it cannot be wrong, others point out that deception undermines the trust that is fundamental to the doctor-patient relationship. Most medical associations today maintain that transparency is essential, even if this means forgoing some of the benefits that placebos can offer.
