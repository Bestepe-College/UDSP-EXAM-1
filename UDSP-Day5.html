VOCABULARY
7 Soru
For questions 1–7, choose the option that best completes the sentence.
1
The committee's decision to approve the budget was met with widespread - - - -, as most stakeholders had expected a much longer review process.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) kayıtsızlık / ilgisizlik
B) şüphecilik / kuşkuculuk
C) şaşkınlık / hayret
D) isteksizlik / çekingenlik
E) coşku / heves
The sentence says stakeholders 'expected a much longer review process' — their expectation was violated, indicating surprise. 'Astonishment' (C) = great surprise, which fits. 'Enthusiasm' would mean they were pleased, not surprised. 'Indifference' = not caring. 'Scepticism' = doubt, not surprise at a decision already made. 'Reluctance' = unwillingness, not a reaction to a surprise.
2
The new workplace policy was carefully designed to - - - - the risk of burnout among employees who regularly work extended hours.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) ağırlaştırmak / kötüleştirmek
B) azaltmak / hafifletmek
C) görmezden gelmek / atlamak
D) güçlendirmek / pekiştirmek
E) sömürmek / istismar etmek
'Carefully designed to [blank] the risk of burnout' implies the policy is protective. 'Mitigate' (B) = reduce or lessen a risk, the standard collocation with 'risk'. 'Aggravate' = make worse (opposite). 'Overlook' = ignore. 'Reinforce' would increase the risk. 'Exploit' = take unfair advantage of.
3
The ancient manuscript was in such a - - - - condition that conservators spent months stabilising it before any restoration work could begin.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) kusursuz / bozulmamış
B) dayanıklı / sağlam
C) tutarlı / bütünlüklü
D) kırılgan / hassas
E) ayrıntılı / karmaşık
The manuscript needed months of stabilisation before any work could begin, implying it was delicate and easily damaged. 'Fragile' (D) = easily broken or damaged, fits perfectly. 'Pristine' = in perfect condition (opposite — no need for stabilisation). 'Durable' = strong and long-lasting (opposite). 'Coherent' relates to logic/meaning. 'Elaborate' = detailed/complex, not about physical condition.
4
Scientists are increasingly concerned that the - - - - effects of microplastics on human health may not become fully apparent for several decades.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) yararlı / faydalı
B) geçici / kısa süreli
C) birikimli / kümülatif
D) öngörülebilir / tahmin edilebilir
E) anlık / hemen gerçekleşen
The sentence says effects 'may not become fully apparent for several decades' — implying they build up slowly over time. 'Cumulative' (C) = increasing in effect through successive additions over time, which is the only option that logically explains a delayed appearance. 'Temporary' contradicts 'decades'. 'Immediate' contradicts 'not apparent for decades'. 'Predictable/beneficial' don't relate to the timeline described.
5
The negotiating team managed to reach a compromise only after making several significant - - - - on issues that had previously been considered non-negotiable.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) talepler / istekler
B) çekinceler / tereddütler
C) taahhütler / yükümlülükler
D) suçlamalar / ithamlar
E) tavizler / ödünler
A compromise is reached by giving something up. 'Concessions' (E) = things you agree to give up in order to reach an agreement — the exact meaning required. 'Demands' = asking for things (opposite direction). 'Accusations' = blaming someone. 'Commitments' = promises to do something. 'Reservations' = doubts or hesitations.
6
The professor's explanation was so - - - - that even students with no prior background in the subject were able to follow every step of the argument.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) teknik / uzmanlık gerektiren
B) açık / anlaşılır
C) muğlak / belirsiz
D) uzun / ayrıntılı
E) teorik / kuramsal
Students with no background could follow the explanation — it was very clear. 'Lucid' (B) = expressed clearly and easy to understand, exactly what is described. 'Ambiguous' = unclear (opposite). 'Technical' = specialised — would make it harder for beginners. 'Lengthy' = long, unrelated to clarity. 'Theoretical' = abstract, would make it harder, not easier.
7
The rewilding project faced - - - - opposition from local farmers, who feared that the reintroduction of predators would threaten their livestock.
Açıklama

B) oybirliği ile / tümünün
C) ılımlı / orta düzeyde
D) önemli / kayda değer
E) zaman zaman / ara sıra
The passage describes 'legitimate concerns' about large predators near farms — implying real, significant opposition. 'Considerable' (D) = large in size or extent, collocates naturally with 'opposition'. 'Unanimous' = everyone agreed (too absolute — passage says 'local farmers', not all people). 'Subtle' = barely noticeable. 'Occasional' = happening from time to time. 'Moderate' = mild, which contradicts fears about livestock safety.
Bölüm 2 — Dil Bilgisi
GRAMMAR
7 Soru
For questions 8–14, choose the answer that best completes each sentence grammatically.
8
By the time the ecological survey - - - -, the rewilding project will have been running for over a decade.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) tamamlayacak (Future — if cümlesinde kullanılmaz)
B) tamamlanmış olacak (Present Perfect)
C) tamamlanıyor (Present Simple Passive)
D) tamamlandığında (Present Simple — zaman zarfı ile) ✓
E) tamamlanıyor olacak (Future Continuous)
In time clauses introduced by 'by the time', 'when', 'before', 'after', English uses Present Simple (not future tense) to refer to future events. The main clause correctly uses 'will have been running' (Future Perfect Continuous). Only 'completes' (D) is a correct Present Simple form here. Using 'will complete' in a time clause is a classic error.
9
The research team insisted that every participant - - - - given the opportunity to withdraw from the study at any point.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) oldu (Past Simple — resmi talep yapısında zayıf)
B) olsaydı (were — subjunctive ama 'be' daha doğru)
C) olsun (base form subjunctive — resmi talep yapısı) ✓
D) olmuştur (Present Perfect)
E) olarak (gerund/participle — yanlış yapı)
After verbs of insistence, demand, or recommendation (insist, demand, require, suggest, propose), formal English uses the bare infinitive subjunctive: 'insist that + subject + BASE VERB'. 'Be' (C) is the correct base form. 'Was/were' would be acceptable only in informal British English. 'Has been' is a tense mismatch. 'Being' is a non-finite form that cannot head a that-clause here.
10
The judge, - - - - ruling later caused significant controversy, had been on the bench for over thirty years.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) hangi / -ki (nesneler için)
B) kim (özne ilgi zamiri — kişiler)
C) kimi (nesne ilgi zamiri)
D) o / ki (tanımlayan yan cümlelerde — virgülle kullanılmaz)
E) kimin / -nın (iyelik ilgi zamiri) ✓
'Ruling' belongs to the judge — this is a possessive relationship. 'Whose' (E) is the possessive relative pronoun, meaning 'the judge's ruling'. 'Who' would need to be the subject of a verb, not followed by 'ruling'. 'Which' refers to things, not people. 'Whom' is the object form for people. 'That' cannot be used in non-defining relative clauses (those set off by commas).
11
The microplastics study would have attracted far greater funding - - - - its initial results been published in a more prominent journal.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) eğer (if — inverted conditional'da düşer)
B) olmadıkça
C) her ne kadar
D) olsaydı (inverted conditional — 'if' düşürülmüş) ✓
E) gerekirse (should — First Conditional inversion'da kullanılır)
This is an inverted Third Conditional — 'if' is omitted and the auxiliary 'had' moves before the subject. 'Had its initial results been published...' = 'If its initial results had been published...'. Only 'had' (D) correctly forms this inverted structure. 'Should' is used in inverted First Conditionals. 'If' cannot be used when the sentence is already inverted.
12
Nutritionists recommend - - - - ultra-processed foods wherever possible, as regular consumption has been linked to several chronic conditions.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) kaçınmak (base form — 'recommend' sonrası yanlış)
B) kaçınmayı (to-infinitive — 'recommend' sonrası yanlış)
C) kaçınmış olmayı (tamamlanmış — yanlış anlam)
D) kaçınılmayı (passive gerund — özne yanlış)
E) kaçınmayı (-ing gerund) ✓
'Recommend' is always followed by the -ing form (gerund) when the subject of the recommending is the same as the one doing the action: 'recommend + -ing'. 'Recommend to avoid' is not standard British English. 'To have avoided' implies a past completed action. 'Avoid' (bare infinitive) cannot follow 'recommend'. 'Being avoided' is passive, but the sentence needs an active meaning.
13
The habitat restoration programme was so underfunded - - - - it could not hire enough rangers to monitor the newly protected areas effectively.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) her ne kadar (zıtlık bağlacı — anlam bozulur)
B) o kadar ki (so...that sonuç yapısı) ✓
C) çünkü / -den beri (zaman/neden — bu yapıyı tamamlamaz)
D) ama (koordinasyon bağlacı — yapıyı tamamlamaz)
E) rağmen (zıtlık — isim grubu gerektirir)
'So + adjective + THAT + result clause' is a fixed result structure. 'So underfunded THAT it could not hire...' — the lack of funding caused the inability to hire rangers. Only 'that' (C) correctly completes this so...that construction. 'Although/but' signal contrast, not result. 'Since' would create a causal clause but the sentence structure requires 'that' here. 'Despite' needs a noun phrase.
14
Neither the government's proposed tax incentives nor the industry's own voluntary commitments - - - - considered sufficient by environmental groups.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) sayılıyor (Present Simple — zaman uyumu belirsiz)
B) sayılıyor (tekil — yakın isimle uyuşmuyor)
C) sayılıyordu (Past Continuous — yanlış zaman)
D) sayıldı (Past Simple tekil — yakın isimle uyuşmuyor)
E) sayılmıştır (Present Perfect çoğul — 'commitments' ile uyumlu) ✓
With 'neither...nor', the verb agrees with the noun CLOSEST to it — here 'commitments' (plural). 'Have been' (E) is the correct Present Perfect plural form. 'Was' and 'is' are singular — they agree with 'tax incentives' (further away), which is wrong. 'Are' is present simple plural and grammatically possible, but 'have been considered' (Present Perfect passive) better reflects an ongoing evaluation in context. 'Were being' is past continuous passive.

For questions 15–24, choose the word or expression that best fits each blank.
15
Behavioural economists have long argued that people are not always rational decision-makers. - - - -, research shows that small environmental changes — such as the order in which options are presented — can have a dramatic impact on the choices people make.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) Buna rağmen (zıtlık)
B) Bunun aksine (doğrudan zıtlık)
C) Tam aksine (doğrudan zıtlık)
D) Gerçekte / Aslında (güçlendirme ve somut kanıt sunma) ✓
E) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
The first sentence makes a general claim (people are not always rational). The second sentence provides specific evidence that supports and strengthens this claim. 'In fact' (D) introduces supporting evidence that reinforces what was just said. 'Nevertheless/In contrast/On the contrary' all signal opposition, but the second sentence does not contradict the first. 'As a result' implies causation, but the second sentence is evidence, not a consequence.
16
The reintroduction of wolves altered the behaviour of elk herds. - - - -, riverside vegetation began to regrow, stabilising the river banks and reducing erosion.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: A
A) Bunun sonucunda / Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç) ✓
B) Bununla birlikte / Ancak (zıtlık)
C) Örneğin (örnek verme)
D) Buna karşılık (zıtlık)
E) Buna ek olarak (ekleme)
The changed behaviour of elk (less overgrazing) directly caused the regrowth of vegetation. 'Consequently' (B) signals a direct causal consequence of what was just described — exactly what the trophic cascade described in the passage illustrates. 'However/By contrast' signal contrast, not causation. 'For instance' would introduce an example, but the second sentence is a direct result. 'In addition' adds new information rather than showing causation.
17
Decision fatigue can affect anyone, regardless of their intelligence or professional training; - - - -, even experienced judges and senior doctors have been shown to make systematically worse decisions later in the day.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) bu nedenle / dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
B) alternatif olarak (seçenek sunma)
C) gerçekte / hatta (güçlendirme ve somut kanıt) ✓
D) buna rağmen (zıtlık)
E) buna karşılık (zıtlık)
The first clause makes a broad claim (affects anyone, including experts). The second clause provides specific evidence to strengthen this claim using high-status examples (judges, doctors). 'In fact' (C) reinforces the claim with concrete evidence and carries the sense of 'and this is true even in these surprising cases'. 'Therefore' would imply causation. 'Nonetheless' signals contrast. 'Alternatively' offers options.
18
Rewilding does not aim to recreate a fixed historical landscape; - - - -, it seeks to restore the natural processes that allow ecosystems to evolve on their own terms.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) buna göre / bu doğrultuda (uyum)
B) üstelik / bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
C) daha ziyade / bunun yerine (düzeltme/açıklama) ✓
D) benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
E) bu arada (eş zamanlılık)
The first clause corrects a possible misconception (rewilding is NOT about a fixed historical recreation). The second clause states what rewilding ACTUALLY aims to do. 'Rather' (C) = 'instead of that / to be more precise' — used to correct or refine what was just said. 'Accordingly' = in a way consistent with what was said (wrong direction). 'Furthermore' = adds more of the same. 'Similarly' = comparison. 'Meanwhile' = at the same time.
19
Microplastics have been found in virtually every environment on Earth, from deep-sea sediments to Arctic ice. - - - -, scientists are still debating the precise threshold at which their concentration becomes harmful to human health.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç — zıtlık değil)
B) Buna ek olarak (ekleme — zıtlık değil)
C) Bu nedenle (neden-sonuç — zıtlık değil)
D) Yani (açıklama/yeniden ifade — zıtlık değil)
E) Ancak / Bununla birlikte (zıtlık) ✓
The first sentence establishes that microplastics are found everywhere (a well-documented fact). The second sentence reveals a limitation — we don't yet know the exact harmful threshold. This is a contrast between what is known and what remains uncertain. 'However' (E) signals this contrast correctly. 'As a result/For this reason' would imply the debate is a consequence of the widespread presence, which doesn't make logical sense.
20
The habit formation process initially requires conscious effort and deliberate practice; - - - -, over time, repeated behaviours become automatic, requiring far less cognitive engagement.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) buna rağmen (zıtlık — ama yanlış bağlam)
B) sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
C) buna karşılık (zıtlık)
D) yani (yeniden ifade)
E) ancak (zıtlık — başlangıç ile zaman içindeki değişimi karşılaştırma) ✓
The contrast is between the beginning of habit formation (effortful) and the later stage (automatic). 'However' (E) signals this temporal contrast between two states of the same process. 'By contrast' works similarly but is less common in this context. 'As a consequence' would imply that the automatic nature is a RESULT of the effort, which is less precise than the contrast intended. 'In other words' would be a restatement, not a contrast.
21
The clinical trial produced promising early results; - - - -, the research team cautioned that far larger studies would be needed before any treatment could be approved.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) bu nedenle (neden-sonuç)
B) buna rağmen / yine de (zıtlık) ✓
C) benzer şekilde (benzerlik)
D) yani (yeniden ifade)
E) hepsinden önemlisi (öncelik/vurgu)
Early results were promising (positive) — the team then urged caution (limiting factor). This is a contrast between the encouraging finding and the need for more evidence before action. 'Nevertheless' (B) = despite this positive result, the caution remains. 'Therefore' would imply the caution is a logical consequence of the good results, which is the wrong relationship. 'Similarly' adds comparable information. 'In other words' restates.
22
AI diagnostic tools have shown impressive accuracy in detecting certain cancers from scan images. - - - -, they currently lack the ability to integrate the broader clinical context that an experienced physician brings to each case.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) Üstelik / Bunun yanı sıra (ekleme)
B) Örneğin (örnek verme)
C) Dolayısıyla (neden-sonuç)
D) Buna rağmen / Yine de (zıtlık) ✓
E) Sonuç olarak (neden-sonuç)
AI tools are impressive (positive) but have a specific limitation (negative). 'Nonetheless' (D) = despite that impressive quality, this limitation exists — correctly signals the concessive contrast. 'Moreover/For example' both continue in the same positive direction. 'Consequently/As a result' would imply the limitation is caused by the accuracy, which is illogical.
23
Many behavioural economists now argue that public policy should account for cognitive limitations, - - - - designing 'nudges' — subtle environmental cues that guide people towards better decisions without restricting their freedom of choice.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) rağmen (zıtlık — isim grubu gerektirir, yanlış bağlam)
B) örneğin (açıklama ve örnekleme) ✓
C) aksine / ile zıt olarak (zıtlık)
D) her ne kadar (zıtlık — tam cümle gerektirir)
E) aksine / yerine (zıtlık)
The sentence explains HOW policy should account for cognitive limitations, then gives a specific method (nudges) as an example. 'For example by' (B) introduces a specific example of the recommended approach with a gerund ('designing nudges'). 'Despite/in contrast to/as opposed to' all signal contrast, which is wrong — the nudge idea supports, not contradicts, the argument. 'Even though' requires a full clause with subject + verb.
24
The concept of rewilding has gained significant traction in conservation circles in recent years, - - - - concerns about its practical implementation in densely populated regions remain unresolved.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) o kadar ki (sonuç yapısı)
B) göz önüne alındığında (neden/koşul)

E) olduğu sürece (koşul)
Rewilding has gained traction (positive) BUT concerns about implementation remain (limiting factor). 'Even though' (D) correctly expresses this concessive contrast between two simultaneous truths. 'So that' = result. 'Given that' = because/considering. 'Provided that' and 'as long as' both introduce conditions for the first clause, which doesn't make logical sense here.

Bölüm 6 — Yakın Anlamlı
REPHRASING
5 Soru
For questions 29–33, find the sentence closest in meaning to the given sentence.
29
It was not until researchers began tracking participants over several years that the true long-term effects of decision fatigue on health outcomes became apparent.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
'It was not until X that Y' = 'Y only happened after X'. B correctly uses 'only became apparent after researchers started tracking' — preserving both the sequence (tracking came first) and the restriction (only then did effects become apparent). A reverses the sequence — says tracking came before studying the effects, and implies prior separate study. C says no significant effects — contradicts original. D says effects were already established — contradicts original. E introduces a specific time limit ('fewer than two years') not in original.
30
The rewilding project attracted barely any opposition from environmental groups, though local farming communities remained deeply divided on the matter.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
'Barely any opposition from environmental groups' = almost no environmental opposition. 'Farming communities remained deeply divided' = strong internal disagreement within farming communities. C correctly preserves both: 'very few environmental groups opposed it' + 'farming communities continued to hold sharply differing views'. A reverses both positions completely. B says both groups were united in opposition — contradicts original (environmental groups barely opposed it). D says widespread opposition from both — contradicts original. E says environmental groups were the MOST vocal opponents — the opposite (barely any opposed).
31
The more cognitively demanding a task is, the more rapidly decision-making quality deteriorates as the day progresses.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
The original uses 'the more...the more' structure: greater cognitive demand → faster deterioration. B correctly captures this proportional relationship: 'declines faster when a task places greater cognitive demands'. A says the two are unrelated — contradicts original. C says the damage is irreversible — not stated. D says the effect is greatest for those with FEWER demanding tasks — reverses the relationship. E introduces sleep recovery — not in original.
32

Passage 1: The Psychology of Decision Fatigue

Every day, humans make thousands of decisions — from trivial choices such as what to eat for breakfast to complex professional judgements that can affect many others. What psychological research has increasingly revealed, however, is that the quality of these decisions does not remain constant throughout the day. As a person makes more decisions, their capacity for careful deliberation gradually diminishes, a phenomenon researchers have termed "decision fatigue."

Studies examining the behaviour of judges, doctors, and financial advisers have consistently demonstrated this effect. In one widely cited piece of research, judges reviewing parole applications were found to grant parole to a significantly higher proportion of applicants in the morning than in the afternoon, with approval rates declining steadily as the day progressed. The researchers concluded that the mental effort required to weigh evidence and reach a balanced verdict was subject to depletion, much like a physical muscle that tires with repeated use.

The consequences of decision fatigue extend beyond professional settings. Consumers shopping in the late afternoon tend to make more impulsive purchases than morning shoppers, while individuals who have spent the day resolving workplace conflicts are more likely to defer even simple personal decisions to the following day. Crucially, people experiencing decision fatigue are often unaware of the decline in their own judgement, believing themselves to be as sharp and focused as they were at the start of the day.

Researchers suggest several strategies for managing this phenomenon. Breaking complex decisions into smaller stages, scheduling high-stakes choices earlier in the day, and simplifying routine decisions by establishing fixed habits are all approaches shown to preserve cognitive resources for when they are most needed.

Passage 2: Rewilding — Restoring Ecosystems

Rewilding is an approach to conservation that goes beyond the protection of individual species to focus on restoring the natural processes and ecological relationships that sustain entire ecosystems. Unlike conventional conservation, which tends to maintain landscapes in a fixed, managed state, rewilding aims to reduce human intervention and allow nature to determine the trajectory of recovery. Its proponents argue that ecosystems, when given sufficient space and the right conditions, possess an inherent capacity to heal themselves.

Central to many rewilding projects is the reintroduction of apex predators and other keystone species — animals whose presence or absence has disproportionate effects on the wider ecosystem. The reintroduction of wolves to Yellowstone National Park in the United States in the 1990s provides one of the most studied examples. Following their return, wolf predation reduced the size and altered the behaviour of elk herds, which had previously overgrazed riverbanks. The resulting regrowth of riverside vegetation stabilised river banks, reduced erosion, and even altered the physical course of several rivers — a cascade of interconnected changes that ecologists described as a "trophic cascade."

Critics of rewilding raise legitimate concerns. Reintroducing large predators into landscapes that now include farms, roads, and dense human populations poses real risks to livestock and, in some cases, to human safety. There is also debate about which historical baseline rewilding projects should aim to recreate, given that ecosystems have never been entirely static and human influence has shaped them for millennia. Despite these challenges, rewilding projects have expanded significantly across Europe and North America, with proponents arguing that at a time of accelerating biodiversity loss, allowing ecosystems to recover on their own terms may represent one of the most cost-effective conservation strategies available.
