Bölüm 2 — Dil Bilgisi
GRAMMAR
7 Soru
For questions 8–14, choose the answer that best completes each sentence grammatically.
8
She - - - - to that gym for three years when it suddenly closed down.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) gidiyor (Present Perfect Continuous)
B) gider (Present Simple)
C) gidiyordu (Past Continuous — süreç ama sıra yok)
D) gitti (Past Simple)
E) gidiyordu (Past Perfect Continuous — geçmişteki daha önceki süregelen eylem) ✓
Two past events: she attended the gym (ongoing), then it closed down (Past Simple). The gym attendance began BEFORE and continued up to the point of closure — Past Perfect Continuous. 'Had been going' (E) shows the duration of the earlier ongoing action relative to the later past event ('closed down'). 'Has been going' (A) = Present Perfect Continuous, connects to now. 'Goes' (B) = Present Simple. 'Was going' (C) = Past Continuous, describes an action in progress at a moment but doesn't establish prior duration. 'Went' (D) = Past Simple, no duration.
9
If I - - - - how difficult the hike was going to be, I would have brought more water and food.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) biliyor (Present Simple)
B) bilseydi (Past Simple — 2. Koşullu)
C) bilmiş olsaydı (Past Perfect — 3. Koşullu) ✓
D) bilmiştir (Present Perfect)
E) bilirdi (would — if cümlesinde kullanılmaz)
'Would have brought' = Third Conditional main clause (unreal past — didn't bring enough water). The if-clause needs Past Perfect: 'If I had known'. 'Had known' (C) correctly completes the Third Conditional — the person didn't know, so didn't bring enough. 'Know' (A) = present — wrong tense for Third Conditional. 'Knew' (B) = Second Conditional (unreal present). 'Have known' (D) = Present Perfect. 'Would know' (E) = 'would' cannot appear in the if-clause.
10
The book - - - - by millions of people around the world since it was first published ten years ago.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) okundu (Past Simple Passive — zaman bağı yok)
B) okunuyordu (Past Simple Passive — anlam kaybı)
C) okunmuştur (Present Perfect Passive — geçmişten bugüne) ✓
D) okur (Present Simple Active — yanlış)
E) okutmaktadır (Present Continuous Active — yanlış)
'Since it was published ten years ago' + an action that has been ongoing up to now = Present Perfect. The passive voice ('by millions') requires 'has been read' (C) = Present Perfect Passive. 'Read' (A) = Past Simple Passive — doesn't connect to the present via 'since'. 'Was read' (B) = Past Simple Passive — same problem. 'Is reading' (E) = Present Continuous Active — wrong voice and tense. 'Reads' (D) = Present Simple Active — wrong voice.
11
You should avoid - - - - heavy meals late at night, as this can make it harder to fall asleep.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: E
A) yemek (to-infinitive — 'avoid' sonrası yanlış)
B) yemek (base form — 'avoid' sonrası yanlış)
C) yedi (Past Simple — yanlış)
D) yenmiş (past participle — yanlış)
E) yemeyi (-ing gerund — 'avoid' sonrası doğru) ✓
'Avoid' always takes the gerund (-ing form): 'avoid + doing something'. 'Eating' (E) is the correct gerund. 'To eat' (A) = infinitive — 'avoid to eat' is ungrammatical. 'Eat' (A) = bare infinitive — also wrong after 'avoid'. 'Ate' (C) = Past Simple. 'Eaten' (D) = past participle — cannot follow 'avoid' here.
12
It is the most beautiful park I - - - - in my life.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) hiç ziyaret ettim (Past Simple — superlative'de yanlış)
B) hiç ziyaret ederim (Present Simple)
C) hiç ziyaret etmiştim (Past Perfect — geçmişte referans noktası gerektirir)
D) hiç ziyaret etmişim (Present Perfect — superlative yapısı) ✓
E) hiç ziyaret ediyordum (Past Continuous)
'The most...I have ever...' is a fixed superlative structure that requires Present Perfect. 'Have ever visited' (D) correctly forms: 'the most beautiful park I have ever visited'. 'Ever visited' (A) = Past Simple — not standard in superlative structures in British English. 'Ever visit' (A) = Present Simple. 'Was ever visiting' (E) = Past Continuous. 'Had ever visited' (C) = Past Perfect, which needs a past reference point ('the most beautiful park I had ever visited before I went to Rome').
13
The cycling club members are looking forward to - - - - the new coastal route next weekend.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) denemek (base form — 'to' preposition sonrası yanlış)
B) denemek (to-infinitive — 'forward to' sonrası yanlış)
C) denedi (Past Simple — yanlış)
D) denemeye (-ing gerund — 'look forward to' sonrası doğru) ✓
E) denemiş olmak (perfect infinitive — yanlış)
'Look forward to' is a phrasal verb where 'to' is a PREPOSITION, not part of an infinitive. After this preposition, the gerund is required: 'look forward to + -ing'. 'Trying' (D) is correct. 'Try' (A) and 'to try' (B) use the infinitive — wrong after a preposition. 'Tried' (C) = Past Simple. 'Have tried' (E) = perfect infinitive.

My neighbour suggested - - - - a street party to celebrate the end of the school year.

'Suggest' always takes the gerund (-ing form): 'suggest + doing something'. 'Organising' (D) is correct. 'To organise' (A) = infinitive — 'suggest to organise' is ungrammatical. Açıklama

Passage 1: The Benefits of Cycling

Cycling is one of the most popular forms of exercise in the world, and for good reason. It is suitable for people of almost all ages and fitness levels, and it puts less pressure on the joints than activities such as running. Beyond the physical benefits, cycling is also good for mental health. Studies have shown that people who cycle regularly tend to feel less stressed and report higher levels of happiness. Being outdoors, breathing fresh air, and moving through the environment at a relaxed pace all contribute to a sense of wellbeing that is difficult to achieve in a gym.

Cycling is also an environmentally friendly way to travel. Unlike cars, bicycles produce no emissions and require no fuel. In many cities, cycling is actually faster than driving during rush hour, as cyclists can avoid traffic jams and use dedicated cycle lanes. Some cities have introduced bicycle-sharing schemes that allow residents and tourists to rent a bike for a short journey, making cycling accessible even to those who do not own one.

Despite these advantages, some people are put off by safety concerns. Cycling in heavy traffic can feel dangerous, particularly for beginners. However, the expansion of cycle lanes and the growing availability of helmets and reflective clothing have made cycling considerably safer in recent years. Most cycling experts agree that the health and environmental benefits of regular cycling far outweigh the risks for the majority of people.
