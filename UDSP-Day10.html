8
I - - - - my driving test twice already, but I am hoping to pass it when I try again next month.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: B
A) başarısız oluyorum (Present Simple)
B) başarısız oldum (Present Perfect — geçmişten bugüne) ✓
C) başarısız oldum (Past Simple — bağlantısız)
D) başarısız olacağım (Future)
E) başarısız oluyorum (Present Continuous)
'Already' + a life experience that is relevant to the present situation = Present Perfect. The person has failed twice and this is relevant to their current situation (trying again next month). 'Have failed' (B) = Present Perfect, the correct tense for experiences up to now that are still relevant. 'Fail' (A) = Present Simple, for habits. 'Am failing' (E) = Present Continuous, for now. 'Failed' (B) = Past Simple — would need a specific time ('I failed last year'). 'Will fail' (D) = future.
9
The train - - - - at platform seven in about ten minutes, so we should make our way there now.
Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: C
A) varıyordu (Past Continuous)
B) varmıştır (Present Perfect)
C) varıyor / varacak (Present Simple — timetable) ✓
D) varmış olacak (Future Perfect)
E) vardı (Past Simple)
Train timetables use Present Simple to describe scheduled future arrivals. 'Arrives' (C) = Present Simple used for fixed, scheduled events in the future ('the train arrives at platform seven in ten minutes'). 'Has arrived' (B) = Present Perfect, for something already completed — but the train hasn't arrived yet. 'Was arriving' (A) = Past Continuous — past and unrelated. 'Will have arrived' (D) = Future Perfect — for completion before a future time. 'Arrived' (B) = Past Simple — past, wrong time.
10

Açıklama
✅ Doğru Cevap: D
A) için (amaç)
B) hakkında (about — surprise bağlamında daha az doğal)
C) den (from — yanlış edat)
D) karşısında (surprised at/by) ✓
E) -in (of — yanlış edat)
'Surprised at' or 'surprised by' are the standard preposition collocations with 'surprised'. 'At' (D) = 'surprised at something' — the correct preposition for describing the source of surprise. 'For' (A) = used for purpose or duration — 'surprised for' is not standard. 'About' (B) = regarding — 'surprised about' is used but less natural than 'surprised at' when describing a direct reaction. 'Of' (E) and 'from' (C) are never used with 'surprised' in this context.

'Suggest' always takes the gerund (-ing form): 'suggest + doing something'. 'Visiting' (D) is correct. 'To visit' (A) = infinitive — 'suggest to visit' is ungrammatical. 'Visit' (A) = bare infinitive — also wrong. 'Visited' (C) = Past Simple. 'To have visited' (E) = perfect infinitive, implies a completed past action.

'Had reached' (C) = Past Perfect — two Past Perfects together ('by the time we had reached, it had closed') is unusual; the time clause should be Past Simple.

Most experts agree that people do not need to travel far to benefit from nature.
